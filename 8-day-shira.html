Shira Route Overview

The 8-Day Shira Route is one of the most breathtaking and less-traveled paths to the summit of Mount Kilimanjaro. Known for its stunning landscapes, excellent acclimatization profile, and remote trails, this route offers a unique and rewarding trekking experience.

What sets the Shira Route apart is its high-altitude start on the Shira Plateau, one of the highest plateaus in the world. This unique beginning allows trekkers to experience the mountain's grandeur from the very first day, with panoramic views of Kibo Peak and the surrounding landscapes.

Why Choose the Shira Route?

  • High-altitude start on Shira Plateau
  • Excellent acclimatization profile
  • Less crowded, remote trails
  • High success rate (90%)
  • Scenic diversity with beautiful vistas
  • "Climb high, sleep low" strategy

Detailed Itinerary

Day-by-day breakdown of your Shira Route adventure

Your adventure begins with a scenic drive from your hotel in Moshi or Arusha to the Shira Gate, located on the western side of Kilimanjaro. The drive takes you through lush farmland and small villages, offering a glimpse of local life and the natural beauty of the region.

The trek starts with a gentle ascent through the heath and moorland zones, surrounded by giant heather and unique high-altitude vegetation. The trail is relatively short, allowing you to ease into the trek and begin acclimatizing. As you hike, you will enjoy stunning views of the Shira Plateau and the distant peak of Kibo.

Key Features:
  • Scenic drive to western side of Kilimanjaro
  • High-altitude start on Shira Plateau
  • Gentle ascent through heath and moorland
  • First night at Simba Camp (3,600m)

After a hearty breakfast, you will continue your ascent across the Shira Plateau, one of the most scenic sections of the trek. The trail offers sweeping views of the surrounding landscape, with Kibo Peak always in sight. As you hike, you will pass through moorland and alpine desert, with the terrain becoming more rugged and dramatic.

The hike is steady but rewarding, with plenty of opportunities to pause and take in the breathtaking scenery. Along the way, your guides will share fascinating insights about the flora, fauna, and history of the area, adding depth to your experience.

Key Features:
  • Trek across scenic Shira Plateau
  • Sweeping views of Kibo Peak
  • Transition to alpine desert terrain
  • Arrival at Shira 2 Camp (3,850m)

Today's trek takes you through the alpine desert, with a gradual ascent to Moir Hut. The trail offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape, with the towering peaks of Kibo and Mawenzi serving as constant backdrops.

The hike is steady but challenging, with the thinning air reminding you of the altitude. Along the way, you will be treated to breathtaking views of the Shira Plateau and the surrounding valleys. By midday, you will arrive at Moir Hut, a remote and peaceful campsite nestled in a serene location.

Key Features:
  • Gradual ascent through alpine desert
  • Stunning views of Kibo and Mawenzi peaks
  • Remote and peaceful Moir Hut location
  • Excellent acclimatization opportunity

Today's trek takes you through the alpine desert, with a stop at Lava Tower for lunch. This "climb high, sleep low" day is crucial for acclimatization, as it helps your body adjust to the altitude. The ascent to Lava Tower is challenging but incredibly rewarding, with the towering rock formation offering a dramatic backdrop for photos.

After lunch, you will descend into the Barranco Valley, a lush and picturesque area surrounded by towering cliffs. The descent is a welcome change, allowing your body to recover while still gaining valuable acclimatization benefits. As you make your way down, you will pass through diverse ecological zones, each with its own unique beauty.

Key Features:
  • "Climb high, sleep low" acclimatization strategy
  • Visit to iconic Lava Tower (4,630m)
  • Descent into scenic Barranco Valley
  • Arrival at Barranco Camp (3,900m)

Day five begins with an exhilarating scramble up the Barranco Wall, a highlight of the trek that offers incredible views and a sense of accomplishment. While the climb may look intimidating at first, your guides will ensure you navigate it safely, and the effort is well worth it for the panoramic views from the top.

Once at the top, you will traverse ridges and valleys, with the trail offering a mix of ascents and descents. The landscape here is rugged and dramatic, with sweeping vistas of the mountain and its glaciers. Along the way, you will be treated to stunning views of Kibo Peak, a constant reminder of the goal that lies ahead.

Key Features:
  • Challenging climb up Barranco Wall
  • Panoramic views from the top
  • Traverse through rugged terrain
  • Arrival at Karanga Camp (3,995m)

Today's trek takes you to Barafu Camp, the base camp for your summit attempt. The trail ascends through barren, rocky terrain, with the summit now clearly in view. The hike is steady but challenging, with the thinning air reminding you of the altitude.

Along the way, you will be treated to stunning views of the surrounding valleys and glaciers, creating a sense of awe and inspiration. By afternoon, you will arrive at Barafu Camp, where your Don Odyssey Safaris team will ensure you are settled in and comfortable.

Key Features:
  • Ascend to base camp at Barafu
  • Stunning views of valleys and glaciers
  • Preparation for summit attempt
  • Early rest for midnight start

Summit day begins around midnight, as you start the steep climb to Uhuru Peak. Guided by headlamps, you will navigate switchbacks and rocky slopes, reaching Stella Point at dawn. From there, it's a final push to the summit, where you will celebrate your achievement with breathtaking views of the sunrise over Africa.

Standing at Uhuru Peak, you will feel a deep sense of pride and accomplishment as you take in the incredible views and snap photos to commemorate your achievement. After celebrating, you will begin the descent, retracing your steps to Barafu Camp for a short break before continuing down to Mweka Camp.

Key Features:
  • Midnight start for summit attempt
  • Sunrise from the roof of Africa
  • Celebration at Uhuru Peak (5,895m)
  • Long descent to Mweka Camp (3,100m)

The final day of your Kilimanjaro adventure is a time for reflection and celebration. After breakfast at Mweka Camp, you will set out on a gentle descent through the rainforest, a stark contrast to the rocky, barren landscapes of the higher altitudes.

After 3 to 4 hours of hiking, you will arrive at Mweka Gate, where your Kilimanjaro adventure officially comes to an end. Here, you will receive your summit certificates and say goodbye to your mountain crew, whose hard work and support were instrumental in your success.

Key Features:
  • Final descent through lush rainforest
  • Receive official summit certificates
  • Celebration with your guide and team
  • Transfer back to your hotel

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the Shira Route

The Shira Route is unique because it starts at a higher elevation on the Shira Plateau (3,600m), offering panoramic views from day one. This route follows the same path as the Lemosho Route for the first two days before joining the Machame Route. It's less crowded than other routes and offers excellent acclimatization opportunities.

While the Shira Route starts at a higher altitude, the 8-day itinerary is designed to allow proper acclimatization. The first day's trek is relatively short, giving your body time to adjust to the altitude. The "climb high, sleep low" strategy used on this route further enhances acclimatization, making it suitable for most trekkers with a good level of fitness.

The best times are during the dry seasons: January to mid-March and June to October. These periods offer the clearest skies and most stable weather conditions. The Shira Route can be particularly spectacular during January and February when the skies are often clear, and the views are breathtaking.
