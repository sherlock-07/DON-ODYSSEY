Machame Route Overview

The 7-Day Machame Route is one of the most scenic and popular paths to summit Mount Kilimanjaro. Known as the "Whiskey Route," it offers trekkers a diverse range of landscapes, from lush rainforests to alpine deserts, culminating in the ultimate challenge of reaching Uhuru Peak, the highest point in Africa.

Why Choose the Machame Route?

  • Excellent acclimatization profile
  • Scenic diversity with beautiful vistas
  • Less crowded than Marangu Route
  • High success rate (92%)
  • Camping experience under the stars
  • Gradual ascent pattern

Quick Facts

  • Route Type Scenic
  • Accommodation Camping

Detailed Itinerary

Day-by-day breakdown of your Machame Route adventure

Your Kilimanjaro adventure begins with an early breakfast at your hotel in Moshi or Arusha, where you will meet your friendly mountain guides and the rest of the crew. After a warm welcome and a brief introduction, you will embark on a scenic drive through the picturesque Tanzanian countryside to the Machame Gate (1,800m).

The first leg of the trek takes you through the enchanting montane rainforest, a lush and vibrant ecosystem teeming with life. As you wind your way up the trail, you will be surrounded by towering trees draped in moss, colorful flowers, and the soothing sounds of birds and wildlife.

Key Features:
  • Dense montane rainforest with ancient trees
  • Possible sightings of blue monkeys and colorful birds
  • Gradual ascent through well-maintained trail

After a restful night at Machame Camp, you will wake up to the crisp mountain air and enjoy a hearty breakfast. Today's trek takes you higher, leaving the lush rainforest behind and transitioning into the striking moorland zone.

As you climb, you will be treated to spectacular panoramas of Kibo Peak, Kilimanjaro's iconic summit, standing majestically in the distance. The moorland's rugged beauty, combined with the ever-changing play of light and shadow across the landscape, makes this day's trek particularly memorable.

Key Features:
  • Transition from rainforest to moorland
  • Spectacular views of Kibo peak
  • First experience with higher altitude

Day three is a pivotal day on your Kilimanjaro adventure, designed to help your body acclimatize to the higher altitudes while treating you to some of the most dramatic scenery on the mountain. After breakfast at Shira Camp, you will set out on a challenging but rewarding trek that follows the principle of "climb high, sleep low."

Your destination for lunch is Lava Tower, a striking volcanic rock formation standing at an impressive 4,630 meters (15,190 feet). This iconic landmark is not only a great spot for a break but also an excellent opportunity for an optional scramble to its summit if you are feeling adventurous and the weather permits.

Key Features:
  • Acclimatization hike to Lava Tower (4,630m)
  • Stunning views of the Western Breach
  • Descent into the scenic Barranco Valley

Day four is an exhilarating and scenic part of your Kilimanjaro journey, designed to further acclimatize your body while preparing you for the summit push ahead. After a hearty breakfast at Barranco Camp, you will face one of the most iconic sections of the Machame Route - the Barranco Wall.

This steep, rocky scramble might look intimidating at first, but with the guidance of your experienced team, you will navigate it with confidence. The climb is both thrilling and rewarding, offering stunning views of the surrounding valleys and peaks as you ascend.

Key Features:
  • Challenging climb up the Barranco Wall
  • Panoramic views of the southern glaciers
  • Short hiking day for better acclimatization

Day five marks a crucial stage in your Kilimanjaro adventure as you make your way to Barafu Camp, the final stop before your summit attempt. After a nourishing breakfast at Karanga Camp, you will set out on a relatively short but steep trek, continuing your circuit around the mountain.

As you climb higher, the landscape becomes increasingly barren and rugged, with the air growing thinner and the temperature cooler. The stark beauty of the alpine desert is both mesmerizing and humbling, reminding you of the challenge that lies ahead.

Key Features:
  • Arrival at base camp for summit attempt
  • Preparation for the midnight summit push
  • Stunning views of Mawenzi and Kibo peaks

The sixth day is the pinnacle of your Kilimanjaro adventure - the day you have been preparing for and dreaming about. It begins in the early hours of the morning, around midnight, when you will wake up, bundle up in your warmest layers, and set out under the cover of darkness.

With headlamps lighting the way, you will begin the slow and steady ascent toward Stella Point, the first major milestone on your way to the summit. The climb is challenging, both physically and mentally, as you navigate steep, rocky terrain in the cold, thin air.

Reaching Stella Point at dawn is an unforgettable moment. The first light of day begins to illuminate the horizon, and you will feel a surge of energy and determination as you push on toward Uhuru Peak, the highest point in Africa at 5,895 meters (19,341 feet).

Key Features:
  • Midnight start for summit attempt
  • Sunrise from the roof of Africa
  • Celebration at Uhuru Peak (5,895m)
  • Long descent to Mweka Camp

The final day of your Kilimanjaro adventure is a time for reflection, celebration, and gratitude. After a restful night at Mweka Camp, you will wake up to the sounds of the rainforest and enjoy a hearty breakfast with your team.

Today's hike is a gentle descent through the lush, green rainforest, a stark contrast to the rocky, barren landscapes of the higher altitudes. As you make your way down the trail, you will pass through dense vegetation, listening to the calls of birds and the rustling of leaves in the breeze.

After 3 to 5 hours of hiking, you will arrive at Mweka Gate, where your Kilimanjaro adventure officially comes to an end. Here, you will receive your summit certificates and say goodbye to your mountain crew, whose hard work and support were instrumental in your success.

Key Features:
  • Final descent through lush rainforest
  • Receive official summit certificates
  • Celebration with your guide and team

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the Machame Route

The best times are during the dry seasons: January to mid-March and June to October. These periods offer the clearest skies and most stable weather conditions. July through September is the most popular time, so book early.

The Machame Route is considered moderately difficult. It's steeper than the Marangu Route but offers better acclimatization. The "climb high, sleep low" profile of Machame makes it more successful than many other routes despite being physically challenging.

You should be in good physical condition with cardiovascular endurance. We recommend regular hiking, running, cycling, or stair climbing for at least 3 months prior to your climb. The better your fitness, the more you'll enjoy the experience.
