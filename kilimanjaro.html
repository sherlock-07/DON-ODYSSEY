Sample Itinerary: Machame Route

7-day climb with optimal acclimatization

Day 1

Machame Gate to Machame Camp

Elevation: 1,800m to 3,000m • Hike Time: 5-7 hours • Habitat: Rainforest

After registration at the gate, begin your climb through lush rainforest to Machame Camp.

Day 2

Machame Camp to Shira Camp

Elevation: 3,000m to 3,840m • Hike Time: 4-6 hours • Habitat: Moorland

Ascend through heather and moorland zones to Shira Plateau with stunning views.

Day 7

Summit Day: Uhuru Peak to Mweka Camp

Elevation: 4,700m to 5,895m (then descend to 3,100m) • Hike Time: 12-16 hours • Habitat: Alpine Desert to Summit

Summit attempt starting at midnight, reaching Uhuru Peak at sunrise. Descend to Mweka Camp for celebration and rest.

Kilimanjaro FAQs

Answers to common questions about climbing Kilimanjaro

The best times are during the dry seasons: January to mid-March and June to October. These periods offer the clearest skies and most stable weather conditions. July through September is particularly popular but also more crowded.

While Kilimanjaro doesn't require technical climbing skills, it's a strenuous hike at high altitude. Success depends more on proper acclimatization and determination than fitness alone. Our guides pace the climb appropriately and monitor everyone for altitude symptoms.
