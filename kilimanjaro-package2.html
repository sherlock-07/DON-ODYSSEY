Trekking FAQs

Answers to common questions about our mountain adventures

How difficult are these climbs and how should I prepare?

Kilimanjaro: Moderate difficulty but requires endurance. Train with 4-6 hour hikes carrying 5kg pack for 2+ months.

Mount Meru: Good for intermediates. Focus on stair climbing and leg strength.

Oldonyo Lengai: Technically challenging - requires scrambling experience. Train on steep, uneven terrain.

All climbs benefit from cardiovascular training and altitude hikes if possible.

When is the best time to climb each mountain?

Kilimanjaro: Jan-Mar & Jun-Oct (Dry seasons). July-Sept busiest.

Mount Meru: Jun-Feb (Avoid heavy rains Apr-May). Dec-Feb clearest views.

Oldonyo Lengai: Jun-Oct (Coolest months). Night climbs year-round.

How do you handle altitude sickness?

Our protocols exceed industry standards: Daily health checks with pulse oximeters, "Pole pole" (slow) pace for proper acclimatization, Extra acclimatization days on Kilimanjaro routes, Emergency oxygen and Gamow bags on all climbs, Immediate descent protocol for severe AMS. Note: Meru's shorter profile makes AMS less common than Kilimanjaro.

What gear do you provide and what should I bring?

We Provide: 4-season mountain tents, Sleeping mats, Dining tent & camp chairs, Emergency oxygen, All cooking equipment.

You Should Bring: Quality sleeping bag (-10°C rating), Layered clothing system, Sturdy broken-in boots, Headlamp (extra batteries), Trekking poles (highly recommended).

Complete packing lists provided after booking.

What are your safety records and guide qualifications?

100% Safety Record, 10+ Years Average Guide Experience, 24/7 Ground Support.

All guides are certified in: Wilderness First Responder (WFR) training, Altitude sickness recognition and treatment, KPAP certified (Kilimanjaro only), English fluency + local language expertise.

Why do trekking costs vary so much between operators?

Key factors affecting price:

Budget Operators: 4:1 client-to-guide ratio, Basic meals, Shared equipment, Minimal safety equipment.

Premium Operators: 2:1 client-to-guide ratio, Chef-prepared meals, High-end private gear, Full emergency systems.

We're transparent about what's included - no hidden porters or national park fees.

What cultural considerations should I know about?

On the Mountain: Always greet crew with "Jambo" or "Mambo", Ask permission before photographing staff, Tip guides/porters collectively (we provide guidelines).

Oldonyo Lengai Specific: Sacred to Maasai - follow guide's instructions carefully, No removing rocks or natural items, Special ceremonies may occur - observe respectfully.
