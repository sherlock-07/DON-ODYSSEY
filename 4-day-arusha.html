Safari Overview

Embark on an unforgettable 4-day safari adventure through Tanzania's iconic destinations: Tarangire National Park, Serengeti National Park, and Ngorongoro Crater. Begin with a thrilling exploration of Tarangire's diverse landscapes and wildlife, including large elephant herds and majestic baobab trees. Venture into the vast plains of the Serengeti, where you will experience breathtaking wildlife encounters and stunning scenery. Conclude with a descent into the awe-inspiring Ngorongoro Crater, home to the Big Five and a host of other fascinating wildlife. This safari promises a perfect blend of thrilling game drives and picturesque vistas, providing a truly immersive Tanzanian experience.

Detailed Itinerary

Day-by-day breakdown of your 4-day safari adventure

Your safari adventure begins with a warm welcome as we pick you up from your hotel in Arusha or Kilimanjaro. Our safari guide will provide a brief overview of the exciting journey ahead. You will then embark on a scenic two-hour drive to Tarangire National Park (three-hour drive if your hotel is in Kilimanjaro). If you need any last-minute items, feel free to ask your guide to stop at a supermarket along the way.

Upon arrival, your guide will take care of the necessary permits, and you will dive right into thrilling game drives. Tarangire is famous for its large elephant herds, and you can also expect to see majestic lions, elusive leopards, zebras, warthogs, wildebeests, monkeys, and elegant giraffes. For bird enthusiasts, the park is a paradise with a wide variety of bird species to spot.

Key Features:
  • Scenic drive through Tanzanian countryside
  • Game drives in Tarangire National Park
  • Large elephant herds and majestic baobab trees
  • Picnic lunch surrounded by stunning scenery
Accommodation Options:

Budget: Ngare Lodge

Midrange: Heart and Soul Lodge

Luxury: The Retreat at Ngorongoro

The next day starts with a hearty breakfast at your lodge, fueling you for an adventurous day ahead. Our professional guide will meet you after breakfast, and you will embark on a picturesque journey to Serengeti National Park. The drive takes you through the charming town of Karatu, where you might catch a glimpse of the local market life. Continuing on, you will traverse the scenic Ngorongoro Highlands, with rolling green hills and vast landscapes that make for a mesmerizing backdrop.

As you approach the Serengeti, the excitement builds. Upon arrival, your guide will take care of the necessary permits while you stretch your legs and enjoy a picnic lunch amidst the stunning surroundings. The Serengeti's vast plains stretch out before you, hinting at the incredible wildlife encounters that await.

After lunch, it's time to dive into the heart of the Serengeti with thrilling game drives. The Park is renowned for its abundance of wildlife. You can expect to see majestic lions lazing in the sun, herds of elephants meandering through the grasslands, and zebras and wildebeests grazing peacefully. Keep your eyes peeled for the elusive leopards that often find refuge in the park's rocky outcrops.

Key Features:
  • Journey through Ngorongoro Highlands
  • Game drives in Serengeti National Park
  • Abundant wildlife including lions, elephants, and leopards
  • Diverse bird species including lilac-breasted rollers
Accommodation Options:

Budget: Robins Camp

Midrange: Safari Haven

Luxury: Serena Safari Lodge

The next day begins with an early breakfast at your lodge, preparing you for another thrilling day of exploration. Our professional guide will then pick you up for more exciting game drives in the Serengeti. As the sun rises, casting a golden glow over the plains, you will set off in search of the park's incredible wildlife.

Throughout the morning, you will traverse the vast landscapes, encountering various animals in their natural habitat. Watch as lions bask in the early morning sun, herds of wildebeests graze peacefully, and elephants make their way through the bush. The diverse ecosystems of the Serengeti provide endless opportunities for fascinating wildlife sightings.

Around midday, you will break for lunch, enjoying a picnic in the heart of the Serengeti. Take this time to relax and soak in the breathtaking surroundings while savoring your meal. After lunch, the adventure continues with a transfer game drive, during which you will still have the chance to spot various animals as you make your way through the park.

As the afternoon progresses, you will begin your journey out of the Serengeti, but the excitement doesn't end there. Keep your eyes peeled for any last-minute wildlife encounters as you traverse the park's diverse terrains. From the grasslands to the woodlands, the Serengeti is full of surprises and incredible sights.

Key Features:
  • Early morning game drives for optimal wildlife viewing
  • Picnic lunch in the heart of Serengeti
  • Transfer game drive through diverse terrains
  • Last-minute wildlife encounters before departure
Accommodation Options:

Budget: Omega View Hotel

Midrange: Marera Valley Lodge

Luxury: Neptune Ngorongoro Luxury Lodge

On the final day of your remarkable adventure, you will start with an early morning breakfast at your accommodation. Our professional guide will then pick you up for a scenic drive to the 8th wonder of the world, the Ngorongoro Crater. Along the way, you will pass through charming towns like Karatu and see Maasai villages nestled in the Ngorongoro Highlands before entering the crater.

Upon arrival at the Ngorongoro gate, your guide will handle all the necessary permits, and you will dive straight into thrilling game drives. Expect to see the iconic Big Five: lions, elephants, buffalo, leopards, and the elusive black rhino. If you are lucky, you might even see them all! In addition to the Big Five, you may encounter other fascinating wildlife such as hyenas, zebras, wildebeest, elands, antelopes, and a variety of bird species.

Game drives will continue until the afternoon, when you will head for a picnic lunch near the hippo pool. Here, you can enjoy your meal while taking in the sights and sounds of these magnificent creatures in their natural habitat. After lunch, the adventure continues with more game drives, allowing you to spot even more wildlife as you explore the crater.

As the evening approaches, you will drive out of the crater and head back to Arusha or to the airport, carrying with you unforgettable memories of this incredible journey. Please note that the time of exiting the crater can vary depending on the distance to your hotel or if you have an early evening flight on the last day.

Key Features:
  • Visit to the Ngorongoro Crater - 8th wonder of the world
  • Opportunity to see the Big Five
  • Picnic lunch near hippo pool
  • Return to Arusha or airport with unforgettable memories

The Great Migration is a year-round phenomenon, but the best time to witness it in the Serengeti is typically from December to July. During this period, you can see the massive herds of wildebeest and zebra moving through the plains. The river crossings in the Northern Serengeti (typically July-October) are a highlight. However, wildlife viewing is excellent throughout the year in Tanzania's national parks. Altezza Travel provides detailed monthly guides to the migration's location.

Our safaris offer a range of accommodations to suit different preferences and budgets. You can choose from comfortable lodge-based safaris (as commonly offered by many operators), authentic tented camps (which can range from classic to luxurious), or a mix of both. We partner with established camps and lodges known for their service, safety, and location. Like Lappet Faced Safaris, we prioritize properties that offer an immersive experience without compromising on comfort.

Yes, children are welcome! Many families enjoy safari together. We can recommend age-appropriate itineraries and accommodations. Some lodges have family suites and pools. A Yellow Fever vaccination certificate is mandatory if traveling from or through a country with risk of transmission. Malaria prophylaxis is highly recommended—consult your doctor. Ensure routine vaccinations are up-to-date (MMR, Hepatitis A & B, Typhoid). We advise all travelers to consult a travel clinic 6-8 weeks before departure. Carrying a basic personal first-aid kit is also a good idea.

Tipping is a customary way to show appreciation for good service. Guidelines are usually provided pre-departure. A common practice is to tip your guide/driver separately from camp/lodge staff (who often have a communal tip box). Amounts are discretionary but often range from $15-25 per guest per day for the guide, and $10-15 per guest per day for general camp staff. Always tip in USD bills.
