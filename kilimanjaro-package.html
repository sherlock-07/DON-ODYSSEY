Kilimanjaro Climbing Experience

5-9 Days Small Groups 90% Success Rate

At 5,895 meters (19,341 feet), Mount Kilimanjaro is Africa's highest peak and the world's tallest free-standing mountain. Our carefully designed climbing itineraries and expert guides give you the best chance of reaching Uhuru Peak while enjoying the journey through Kilimanjaro's stunning ecological zones.

Location

Northern Tanzania, near Moshi

Best Time to Climb

January-March and June-October

Temperature Range

+25°C to -20°C (base to summit)

Machame Route Itinerary

7-day "Whiskey Route" with excellent acclimatization

1

Machame Gate to Machame Camp

Elevation: 1,800m to 3,000m
Hike Time: 5-7 hours
Habitat: Rainforest

After registration at the gate, begin your climb through lush rainforest to Machame Camp.

2

Machame Camp to Shira Camp

Elevation: 3,000m to 3,840m
Hike Time: 4-6 hours
Habitat: Moorland

Ascend through heather and moorland zones to Shira Plateau with stunning views.

3

Shira Camp to Barranco Camp

Elevation: 3,840m to 3,950m
Hike Time: 5-7 hours
Habitat: Alpine Desert

Hike to Lava Tower (4,630m) for acclimatization before descending to Barranco Camp.

4

Barranco Camp to Karanga Camp

Elevation: 3,950m to 4,035m
Hike Time: 3-5 hours
Habitat: Alpine Desert

Conquer the Barranco Wall and traverse valleys to Karanga Camp.

5

Karanga Camp to Barafu Camp

Elevation: 4,035m to 4,640m
Hike Time: 3-5 hours
Habitat: Alpine Desert

Ascend to base camp at Barafu, resting early for summit attempt.

6

Summit Day: Uhuru Peak to Mweka Camp

Elevation: 4,640m to 5,895m (then descend to 3,100m)
Hike Time: 12-16 hours
Habitat: Alpine Desert to Summit

Summit attempt starting at midnight, reaching Uhuru Peak at sunrise. Descend to Mweka Camp.

7

Mweka Camp to Mweka Gate

Elevation: 3,100m to 1,640m
Hike Time: 3-5 hours
Habitat: Rainforest

Final descent through rainforest to receive summit certificates.
