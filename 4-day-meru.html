Mount Meru 4-Day Trek Overview

Join us for an exhilarating 4-day trek to conquer the majestic Mount Meru, Tanzania's hidden gem. This adventure promises breathtaking landscapes, diverse wildlife, and unforgettable memories.

Begin your journey with a scenic drive from Arusha to the Momella Gate, trekking through lush montane forests, home to colobus monkeys and elephants. Enjoy the stunning views as you reach Miriakamba Hut for your first night.

Detailed Itinerary

Day-by-day breakdown of your Mount Meru adventure

In the morning, you will be picked up from your hotel. After a 45-minute drive, you will arrive at the Arusha National Park Gate for entry formalities. Afterwards, we drive a little further to Momella Gate to start our climb.

The trek begins through open grasslands, offering your first taste of wildlife viewing. Keep an eye out for buffalo, warthogs, and elephants grazing in the distance as you enjoy the steady climb through lush montane forest.

Key Features:
  • Wildlife viewing in open grasslands
  • Lunch at the famous "Fig Tree"
  • First views of Mount Meru's cliffs and Ash Cone

Begin your day with a hearty breakfast at Miriakamba Hut before embarking on your trek to the saddle below Little Meru. The journey starts through attractive, open, and lush montane forest. As you ascend, the path becomes steeper and more challenging.

By mid-morning, you will reach Elephant Ridge, the halfway point of today's trek. Here, you can take a short break to catch your breath and enjoy the panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes.

Key Features:
  • Elephant Ridge viewpoint
  • Transition from forest to moorland vegetation
  • Optional climb to Little Meru for sunset views

An early start at around 2:00 AM sets the tone for an exhilarating day ahead. You will begin your ascent from Saddle Hut, climbing steeply towards Rhino Point (3,800m). The path continues along an undulating ridge of ash and rock, challenging but rewarding.

As you approach Cobra Point (4,350m) around sunrise, you will be greeted with stunning views: the cliffs of the crater rim, the Ash Cone rising from the crater floor, and Kilimanjaro floating on the morning clouds.

Key Features:
  • Sunrise from Cobra Point
  • Summit at Socialist Peak (4,566m)
  • Panoramic views of Kilimanjaro and Rift Valley

On the final day of your adventure, you will embark on a fast descent through open grassland and mixed forest, with good chances of seeing wildlife along the way. As you descend, enjoy excellent views back towards the crater and over the vast plains of the National Park.

The trail to Momella Gate takes about 2 to 3 hours of walking. There are two routes you can take down: either retrace your steps along the path you came up, or walk down the jeep road, which descends to the lower crater floor.

Key Features:
  • Fig Tree Arch - impressive natural feature
  • Hidden waterfall visit
  • Final wildlife viewing opportunities

Wildlife on Mount Meru

Encounter diverse wildlife during your trek

Black-and-White Colobus Monkeys

These distinctive primates with flowing white fur are commonly seen in the forested areas of Mount Meru.

Elephants

Often spotted grazing in the open grasslands, especially in the lower sections of the mountain.

Buffalo

Frequently seen in groups, particularly in the forested sections and near water sources.

Diverse Birdlife

Over 400 species of birds inhabit the various ecosystems of Mount Meru, making it a birdwatcher's paradise.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about climbing Mount Meru

Yes, Mount Meru is an excellent acclimatization trek before attempting Kilimanjaro. The 4-day itinerary allows for proper altitude adaptation, and many climbers use it as preparation for the higher altitudes of Kilimanjaro.

Mount Meru is home to diverse wildlife including buffalo, elephants, warthogs, black-and-white colobus monkeys, blue monkeys, and over 400 species of birds. The presence of wildlife is why armed rangers accompany all treks.

Mount Meru can be climbed year-round, but the best conditions are during the dry seasons: January to mid-March and June to October. These periods offer clearer skies and better visibility for enjoying the spectacular views.
