Kilimanjaro Climbing

Standing at 5,895 meters (19,341 feet), Mount Kilimanjaro is Africa's highest peak and the world's tallest free-standing mountain. Our carefully designed climbing itineraries and expert guides give you the best chance of reaching Uhuru Peak while enjoying the journey through Kilimanjaro's stunning ecological zones.

With Don Odyssey Safaris, you'll benefit from:

  • 90%+ summit success rate
  • Experienced, certified guides
  • Small group sizes (max 10 climbers)
  • High-quality camping equipment
  • Comprehensive pre-climb briefing
  • Focus on safety and acclimatization

Sample Kilimanjaro Itinerary

7-day Machame Route

Day 1

Machame Gate to Machame Camp

Elevation: 1,800m to 3,000m • Hike Time: 5-7 hours • Habitat: Rainforest

After registration at the gate, begin your climb through lush rainforest to Machame Camp.

Day 2

Machame Camp to Shira Camp

Elevation: 3,000m to 3,840m • Hike Time: 4-6 hours • Habitat: Moorland

Ascend through heather and moorland zones to Shira Plateau with stunning views.

Day 7

Summit Day: Uhuru Peak to Mweka Camp

Elevation: 4,700m to 5,895m (then descend to 3,100m) • Hike Time: 12-16 hours • Habitat: Alpine Desert to Summit

Summit attempt starting at midnight, reaching Uhuru Peak at sunrise. Descend to Mweka Camp for celebration and rest.

Mount Meru Climbing Adventures

At 4,562 meters (14,968 feet), Mount Meru is Tanzania's second highest peak and an excellent preparation climb for Kilimanjaro. Located in Arusha National Park, it offers stunning views of Kilimanjaro and unique wildlife encounters during the ascent.

Why climb Meru with us:

  • 85% summit success rate
  • Shorter duration (3-4 days)
  • Wildlife viewing opportunities
  • Less crowded than Kilimanjaro
  • Spectacular ash cone and crater views
  • Ideal acclimatization for Kilimanjaro

Oldoinyo Lengai Climbing Adventures

Known as the "Mountain of God" to the Maasai, Oldoinyo Lengai (2,962m/9,718ft) is Africa's only active volcano that erupts natrocarbonatite lava. This unique night climb offers an unforgettable adventure with sunrise views over the Rift Valley.

Why climb Lengai with us:

  • 80% summit success rate
  • Night ascent to avoid daytime heat
  • Witness active volcanic features
  • Spectacular Rift Valley views
  • Unique cultural significance
  • Combined with Lake Natron visit

Trekking FAQs

Answers to common questions about our mountain adventures

Kilimanjaro: Moderate difficulty but requires endurance. Train with 4-6 hour hikes carrying 5kg pack for 2+ months.

Mount Meru: Good for intermediates. Focus on stair climbing and leg strength.

Oldonyo Lengai: Technically challenging - requires scrambling experience. Train on steep, uneven terrain.

All climbs benefit from cardiovascular training and altitude hikes if possible.
