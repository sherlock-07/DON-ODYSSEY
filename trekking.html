Trekking FAQs

Answers to common questions about our mountain adventures

Kilimanjaro: Moderate difficulty but requires endurance. Train with 4-6 hour hikes carrying 5kg pack for 2+ months.

Mount Meru: Good for intermediates. Focus on stair climbing and leg strength.

Oldonyo Lengai: Technically challenging - requires scrambling experience. Train on steep, uneven terrain.

All climbs benefit from cardiovascular training and altitude hikes if possible.

Our protocols exceed industry standards:
  • Daily health checks with pulse oximeters
  • "Pole pole" (slow) pace for proper acclimatization
  • Extra acclimatization days on Kilimanjaro routes
  • Emergency oxygen and Gamow bags on all climbs
  • Immediate descent protocol for severe AMS
Note: Meru's shorter profile makes AMS less common than Kilimanjaro

We Provide:
  • 4-season mountain tents
  • Sleeping mats

You Should Bring:

On the Mountain:
  • Always greet crew with "Jambo" or "Mambo"
  • Ask permission before photographing staff
  • Tip guides/porters collectively (we provide guidelines)
Oldonyo Lengai Specific:
  • Sacred to Maasai - follow guide's instructions carefully
  • No removing rocks or natural items
  • Special ceremonies may occur - observe respectfully
