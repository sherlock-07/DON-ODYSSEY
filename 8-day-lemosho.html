Lemosho Route Overview

The 8-Day Lemosho Route is one of the most scenic and rewarding ways to climb Mount Kilimanjaro. Known for its breathtaking landscapes, excellent acclimatization profile, and lower crowds, this route offers a perfect blend of adventure, challenge, and natural beauty.

Why Choose the Lemosho Route?

  • Excellent acclimatization profile
  • Scenic diversity with beautiful vistas
  • Less crowded than other routes
  • High success rate (95%)
  • Gradual ascent pattern
  • Diverse ecological zones

Quick Facts

  • Route Type Scenic
  • Accommodation Camping
  • Group Size 2-12 people
  • Difficulty Moderate
  • Success Rate 95%
  • Duration 8 Days
  • Summit Height 5,895m

Detailed Itinerary

Day-by-day breakdown of your Lemosho Route adventure

Your Kilimanjaro journey starts with an early breakfast at your hotel in Arusha, followed by a comprehensive briefing from your guides. Afterward, you will embark on a scenic drive through the Tanzanian countryside to Lemosho Park Gate. The trek begins with a gentle walk through the enchanting montane rainforest, shaded by towering trees draped with moss.

Key Features:
  • Scenic drive through Tanzanian countryside
  • Gentle walk through montane rainforest
  • Possible sightings of colobus monkeys

Today's trek takes you higher, leaving the lush rainforest behind and transitioning into the striking moorland zone. The trail begins with a gradual ascent, offering a chance to warm up your legs and soak in the changing scenery. As you climb, the dense forest gives way to open vistas, with the landscape transforming into a sea of giant heather and other unique high-altitude vegetation.

Key Features:
  • Transition from rainforest to moorland
  • Panoramic views of Shira Plateau
  • Unique high-altitude vegetation

Day three is a gentle and scenic day designed to help you acclimatize while exploring the stunning Shira Plateau. After breakfast at Shira 1 Camp, you will set out on a leisurely hike across the plateau, enjoying the expansive views and unique moorland landscapes. The trail is relatively flat, making it a pleasant and relaxing walk as you soak in the beauty of your surroundings.

Key Features:
  • Leisurely hike across Shira Plateau
  • Excellent acclimatization day with minimal elevation gain
  • Sweeping vistas of Kibo Peak and surrounding valleys
  • Optional short hikes for further exploration

Day four is a pivotal day on your Kilimanjaro adventure, designed to help your body acclimatize while treating you to some of the most dramatic scenery on the mountain. The trail gradually ascends to the east, following a ridge that offers stunning views. The highlight of the day is reaching Lava Tower (4,650m), a striking volcanic rock formation known as the "Shark's Tooth".

This "climb high, sleep low" day is crucial for acclimatization, as spending time at this higher altitude before descending helps your body adjust to the thinning air. After lunch, the trail descends into the picturesque Barranco Valley, a lush area that contrasts sharply with the barren landscapes of the higher altitudes.

Key Features:
  • Visit to Lava Tower (4,650m) for acclimatization
  • "Climb high, sleep low" strategy for better adaptation
  • Dramatic change in landscapes from alpine desert to lush valleys
  • First views of the iconic Barranco Wall

Day five begins with an exhilarating climb as you tackle the iconic Barranco Wall, one of the most thrilling sections of the Lemosho Route. This steep but manageable scramble offers breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys and glaciers. While it may look intimidating at first, your guides will ensure you navigate it safely.

Once you've conquered the Barranco Wall, the trail levels out and takes you through a series of ridges and valleys with rugged, dramatic landscapes and sweeping vistas of the mountain and its glaciers. The ups and downs of the trail are crucial for acclimatization, helping your body adapt to the altitude.

Key Features:
  • Climb the iconic Barranco Wall (non-technical scramble)
  • Panoramic views of surrounding valleys and glaciers
  • Continued acclimatization with varied terrain
  • Shorter hiking day to prepare for higher altitudes

Day six is a crucial stage in your Kilimanjaro adventure as you make your way to Barafu Camp, the base camp for your summit attempt. The trail ascends through the alpine desert, a stark and barren landscape that feels almost otherworldly. As you climb, you'll complete the Southern Circuit, offering stunning views of the summit from multiple angles.

This part of the trek is both challenging and rewarding, with the thinning air reminding you of the altitude while the breathtaking scenery inspires you to keep going. Upon arrival at Barafu Camp, the afternoon is dedicated to rest and preparation for the midnight summit attempt.

Key Features:
  • Ascend to high-altitude base camp (4,700m)
  • Complete the Southern Circuit with panoramic summit views
  • Rest and preparation for summit night
  • Early dinner and attempt to sleep before midnight start

Day seven is the pinnacle of your Kilimanjaro adventure - summit day! Beginning around midnight, you'll bundle up in your warmest layers and set out under the cover of darkness with headlamps lighting the way. The climb is challenging both physically and mentally as you navigate steep, rocky terrain in the cold, thin air.

Reaching Stella Point at dawn is an unforgettable moment, with the first light illuminating the horizon. From there, it's a final push to Uhuru Peak (5,895m), the highest point in Africa. Standing at the summit with the sun rising over the clouds is a moment of pure triumph.

After celebrating your success, you'll begin the long descent, retracing your steps through alpine desert and moorland zones, eventually reaching the lush greenery of the rainforest at Mweka Camp.

Key Features:
  • Midnight start for summit attempt
  • Reach Stella Point at dawn
  • Summit Uhuru Peak - highest point in Africa (5,895m)
  • Breathtaking sunrise views from the roof of Africa
  • Long descent to Mweka Camp through multiple ecological zones

The final day of your Kilimanjaro adventure is a time for reflection, celebration, and gratitude. After a restful night at Mweka Camp, you will wake up to the sounds of the rainforest and enjoy a hearty breakfast with your team. Today's hike is a gentle descent through the lush, green rainforest, a stark contrast to the rocky, barren landscapes of the higher altitudes.

Key Features:
  • Gentle descent through lush rainforest
  • Receive official summit certificates
  • Celebration with your guide and team

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the Lemosho Route

The Lemosho Route is known for its scenic beauty, excellent acclimatization profile, and lower crowds. It approaches the mountain from the west, offering stunning views and a more remote experience compared to other routes.

While the Lemosho Route has a high success rate due to its excellent acclimatization profile, it's still a challenging trek. Beginners with good physical fitness can attempt it, but proper preparation is essential.

The best times are during the dry seasons: January to mid-March and June to October. These periods offer the clearest skies and most stable weather conditions.
