Safari Frequently Asked Questions

Expert insights to help you plan your perfect African adventure

Trip Planning

Dry Season (June-October): Best for wildlife viewing as animals congregate around water sources. Excellent for photography with clear skies.

Wet Season (November-May): Lush landscapes, fewer tourists, and fantastic for bird watching. January-February is calving season in some regions.

Migration Viewing: Varies by location - generally July-October for river crossings in East Africa.

Pro Tip: Consider shoulder seasons (April-May or November) for better rates and fewer crowds.

Essentials:
  • Neutral-colored clothing (khaki, green, beige)
  • Lightweight, breathable fabrics
  • Warm layer for morning/evening game drives
  • Comfortable closed-toe shoes
  • Sun hat and polarized sunglasses
Gear & Accessories:
  • Binoculars (8x42 or 10x42 recommended)
  • Camera with zoom lens (200mm minimum)
  • Power bank and universal adapter
  • Reusable water bottle
  • Small daypack
Health & Safety

Safari game drives are very safe when conducted by licensed operators. Your safety is ensured through:

Expert Guides

Professionally trained and certified

Proper Vehicles

Specially modified 4x4 safari vehicles

Strict Protocols

Clear safety rules and procedures

Important: Always remain in the vehicle during game drives unless your guide indicates it's safe to exit.

Vaccination Required/Recommended Notes
Yellow Fever Required Certificate needed for entry in many African countries
Malaria Prophylaxis Highly Recommended Consult your doctor 4-6 weeks before travel
Hepatitis A & B Recommended Standard travel vaccinations
Typhoid Recommended Especially if visiting rural areas

Note: Always consult with a travel medicine specialist 6-8 weeks before your trip.

Safari Experience

Recommended Gear:
  • Camera Body: DSLR or mirrorless with good low-light performance
  • Lenses: 70-200mm f/2.8 (minimum), 100-400mm or 150-600mm ideal
  • Binoculars: 8x42 for general use, 10x42 for more reach
  • Accessories: Extra batteries, memory cards, lens cleaning kit
  • Bag: Dust-proof camera bag or backpack

Bean bags are often provided in safari vehicles for stable shooting

Travel Logistics

International Flights

Standard airline restrictions apply (usually 23kg checked, 7-10kg cabin)

Safari Light Aircraft

Strict limits: 15-20kg total in soft-sided bags (no hard suitcases)

Dimensions: Usually 25cm x 30cm x 62cm maximum

Pro Tip: Pack in soft duffel bags for safari flights and consider leaving extra luggage at your arrival hotel.

For Children

Many lodges welcome children with age-appropriate activities. Consider:

  • Private vehicle for flexibility
  • Lodges with family suites and pools
  • Shorter game drives
  • Minimum age restrictions for walking safaris
For Seniors

Safaris can be enjoyed at any age with proper planning:

  • Lodge-based safaris with fewer moves
  • Properties with medical facilities nearby
  • Limited walking required
  • Private guides for personalized pace
