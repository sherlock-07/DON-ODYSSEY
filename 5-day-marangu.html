Marangu Route Overview

The Marangu Route, often called the "Coca-Cola Route," is one of Kilimanjaro's most popular paths. This 5-day itinerary offers comfortable hut accommodations as you ascend through diverse ecological zones: rainforest, heath-moorland, alpine desert, and the arctic summit zone.

Why Choose the Marangu Route?

  • Comfortable hut accommodations
  • Shorter duration (5 days)
  • Well-maintained trails
  • Gradual ascent pattern
  • Only route with sleeping huts
  • Classic Kilimanjaro experience

Quick Facts

  • Route Type Classic
  • Accommodation Mountain Huts
  • Group Size 2-12 people
  • Difficulty Moderate
  • Success Rate 85%
  • Duration 5 Days
  • Summit Height 5,895m

Detailed Itinerary

Day-by-day breakdown of your Marangu Route adventure

Your adventure begins with an early morning pickup from your hotel in Arusha/Moshi. After meeting your guides, chefs, and porters, you'll drive to the Marangu Gate. After registration, you'll begin your trek through the evergreen rainforest, a lush ecosystem teeming with life. Keep an eye out for colobus monkeys and colorful bird species.

After a few hours of hiking, you'll stop for a picnic lunch at Kisambioni, the halfway point. Refreshed and reenergized, you'll continue your ascent through the forest, gradually gaining elevation to reach Mandara Hut (2,700 meters).

Key Features:
  • Lush rainforest with diverse wildlife
  • Comfortable A-frame hut accommodations
  • Stunning views of Mawenzi Peak on clear days

After breakfast at Mandara Hut, you'll begin your journey to Horombo Hut. Today's trek takes you through a dramatic change in landscape as you leave the lush rainforest behind and enter the moorland zone. This rocky desert plain is dotted with unique succulent plants and offers stunning views.

You'll stop for a picnic lunch at Kambi ya Taabu, with breathtaking views of Mawenzi Peak to the east and Kibo Peak to the west. By mid-afternoon, you'll arrive at Horombo Hut (3,720 meters), known as the best huts on Mount Kilimanjaro.

Key Features:
  • Transition from rainforest to moorland
  • Spectacular views of both Kibo and Mawenzi peaks
  • Comfortable A-frame huts with capacity for 8 people each

After breakfast at Horombo Hut, you'll set out through the moorland and into the alpine desert region. The landscape changes dramatically as lush greenery gives way to stark, otherworldly beauty.

You'll encounter the Maua River, then continue past Middle Red Hill where you'll stop for lunch. The trail then continues through Kilimanjaro's saddle, a vast barren expanse between Kibo and Mawenzi peaks, offering unparalleled views.

By afternoon, you'll arrive at Kibo Hut (4,703 meters), your base for the summit attempt. After settling in, rest as much as possible, hydrate well, and prepare for the midnight climb to the summit.

Key Features:
  • Transition to alpine desert landscape
  • Trek through Kilimanjaro's surreal saddle
  • Final preparation before summit attempt

The pinnacle of your journey begins shortly after midnight. Trekking under a starlit sky, you'll ascend to Gillman's Point (5,681 meters) on the crater rim - a strenuous 5-hour hike considered the least difficult of the three crater ascent paths.

At Gillman's Point, you'll be rewarded with a magnificent sunrise. From here, it's a 2-hour hike along the crater rim to Uhuru Peak (5,895 meters), the Roof of Africa. Celebrate your achievement and soak in the panoramic views.

After descending to Kibo Hut for a short rest, you'll continue down to Horombo Hut (3,720 meters) for a well-deserved night's rest.

Key Features:
  • Midnight start for summit attempt
  • Sunrise from Gillman's Point
  • Celebration at Uhuru Peak, Africa's highest point
  • Long but rewarding descent

After breakfast at Horombo Hut, you'll begin your descent to the Marangu Gate through Mount Kilimanjaro's tranquil cloud forest. The trail winds through lush greenery, with sunlight filtering through the canopy.

You'll pass through Mandara Hut, where you spent your first night, and continue down through the rainforest. The descent is a time to reflect on your achievements and celebrate your success.

Upon reaching the Marangu Gate, you'll receive your summit certificates and have the opportunity to purchase souvenirs. Our vehicle will then drive you back to your hotel in Arusha/Moshi.

Key Features:
  • Peaceful descent through cloud forest
  • Receive official summit certificates
  • Celebration with your guide and team
  • Transfer back to your hotel

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the Marangu Route

The Marangu Route is unique because it's the only route that offers sleeping huts instead of tents. These huts provide basic but comfortable accommodations with beds and mattresses. It's also known as the "Coca-Cola Route" and is popular for its shorter duration (5 days) and well-maintained trails.

The Marangu Route has a slightly lower success rate (around 85%) primarily because of its shorter duration. The 5-day itinerary provides less time for acclimatization compared to longer routes. We recommend considering an extra day for better acclimatization if possible.

The huts are basic but comfortable A-frame structures. Mandara Hut can accommodate up to seventy people, Horombo Hut up to 120 people, and Kibo Hut has capacity for about sixty people. They provide bunk beds with mattresses. There are communal dining halls and basic toilet facilities at each hut.
