Ol Donyo Lengai Overview

Ol Doinyo Lengai, meaning "Mountain of God" in the Maasai language, is the only active volcano on Earth that erupts natrocarbonatite lava. This unique geological wonder features lava temperatures of approximately 500°C—significantly lower than typical volcanoes—creating a once-in-a-lifetime trekking experience.

Detailed Itinerary

Day-by-day breakdown of your Ol Donyo Lengai adventure

Your adventure begins early in the morning as your professional driver-guide picks you up from your hotel in Arusha for a scenic drive to Lake Natron. Along the way, you will pass through traditional Maasai villages, vast savannah plains, and the dramatic escarpments of the Great Rift Valley.

Upon arrival, you will visit Lake Natron, a surreal soda lake known as the primary breeding ground for lesser flamingos. The lake's high alkalinity creates a striking red and pink hue, attracting thousands of flamingos. Afterward, you will have the chance to interact with the local Maasai community, learning about their traditions and spiritual connection to the "Mountain of God."

As the day winds down, enjoy a hearty dinner at your lodge or campsite and relax as you prepare for the thrilling nighttime ascent of Ol Doinyo Lengai, which begins around midnight.

Key Features:
  • Scenic drive through Great Rift Valley
  • Visit Lake Natron flamingo breeding grounds
  • Maasai cultural interaction
  • Preparation for nighttime ascent

Your second day begins in the early hours as you continue your climb to the summit of Ol Doinyo Lengai, aiming to reach the top in time to witness the spectacular sunrise over the Great Rift Valley. Standing atop this unique active volcano, you will be rewarded with 360-degree panoramic views.

After exploring the summit, you will begin your descent. The adventure continues with a refreshing stop at the waterfalls near Lake Natron, where you can cool off with a swim in the natural pools. Back at the campsite, a hot lunch awaits before you embark on the scenic drive back to Arusha.

Key Features:
  • Nighttime ascent for cooler temperatures
  • Spectacular sunrise from summit
  • 360° views of Great Rift Valley
  • Visit to waterfalls near Lake Natron
  • Return to Arusha with unforgettable memories

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the Ol Donyo Lengai Trek

The climb starts at midnight to avoid the scorching daytime temperatures, making the hike more comfortable and safer. The cooler night air also adds a sense of mystery and excitement as you trek under a canopy of stars with the glowing silhouette of the volcano ahead.

Ol Donyo Lengai is the only active volcano on Earth that erupts natrocarbonatite lava. This unique lava has a much lower temperature (about 500°C) compared to typical silicate lavas (1,100°C). It's also much more fluid and appears black during the day but glows orange at night.

The Ol Donyo Lengai trek is challenging and requires a good level of fitness. The ascent is steep and involves some scrambling. We recommend regular cardiovascular exercise and leg strength training in the weeks leading up to your trek. The nighttime ascent also requires mental preparedness.
