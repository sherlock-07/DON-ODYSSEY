Safari Destinations

Explore Tanzania's most iconic national parks

Serengeti National Park

World-famous for the Great Migration

Witness millions of wildebeest and zebra traverse the plains in search of fresh grazing, along with predators like lions and cheetahs.

Highlights:

  • Great Migration (seasonal)
  • Big Five sightings
  • Hot air balloon safaris
  • Endless savanna vistas

Ngorongoro Crater

The world's largest intact caldera

Descend into this natural amphitheater teeming with wildlife, including the rare black rhino and large lion prides.

Highlights:

  • Highest density of predators in Africa
  • Black rhino sightings
  • Flamingo-filled Lake Magadi
  • Maasai cultural experiences
Inquire Now

Tarangire National Park

Elephant paradise with baobab-dotted landscapes

Known for its large elephant herds and iconic baobab trees, Tarangire offers spectacular dry-season game viewing.

Highlights:

  • Large elephant herds (up to 300)
  • Ancient baobab trees
  • Excellent birdwatching
  • Less crowded than Serengeti

Safari FAQs

Answers to common questions about our safaris

The best wildlife viewing is during the dry seasons (June-October and December-February). The Great Migration is typically in Serengeti from December-July, with river crossings in the north around August-September. January-February is calving season in the southern Serengeti.

Essentials include neutral-colored clothing, a wide-brimmed hat, sturdy walking shoes, binoculars, sunscreen, a camera with zoom lens, and light layers for cool mornings. We provide a detailed packing list upon booking.
